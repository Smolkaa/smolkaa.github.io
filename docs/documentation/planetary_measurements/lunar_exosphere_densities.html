3.2 Lunar Exosphere Surface Densities

Author

Alexander Smolka, a.smolka@tum.de
Technical University of Munich

Published

August 3, 2023

This section lists referenced values of the lunar exosphere surface densities, generally provided as a number density in \(\left[\,\mathrm{cm^{\mathrm{-3}}}\right]\). Some references are providing their values in terms of a column density in \(\left[\,\mathrm{cm^{\mathrm{-2}}}\right]\), which includes the respectives measurements coulumn height, i.e., the illuminated line of sight of brightness measurements like LAMP.

See the additional information section on this section’s appendix to find details about some sources, their assumptions, and their measurement methods.

Additional Information

  • Gladstone et al. (2009) assume for the LAMP derivations a \(30\,\mathrm{min}\), \(3\sigma\) observation, and a path length of \(300\,\mathrm{km}\).
  • Hodges et al. (1973) assumed a solar wind proton influx of \(3 \times 10^8 \,\mathrm{s^{\mathrm{-1}}cm^{\mathrm{-3}}}\) for their simulations.
  • CHACE measurements were made during a Moon’s pass through Earth’s magnetotail, reducing the solar wind influx of particles dramatically.
  • Heiken et al. (1991) reported a joint upper limit of \(0.5 \times 10^0\) for both \(\ce{OH}\) and \(\ce{H2O}\)
